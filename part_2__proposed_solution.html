All the location potentials tend to stabilize after a certain amount of time. It is appreciable that around 40 ms the membrane potential has reach the stationary value thus it does not change significatly in any of the locations. The curve seen in all the locations follow a impulse response of a first order system. This is what is expected from a circuit like the one we are modelling because it is composed of resistors and capacities. As we go further from the point of the clamp, the stationary value of the membrane potential decreases, logically, taking into account the capacitors. Nevertheless, the time constant remains the same.

The peak is smoother as the location gets further from the point of injection. This is due to the fact that the sudden change in intensity of current within the capacitances of the membrane becomes a peak on the voltage but as the intensity goes through the nerve it becomes less sharped the change due to the fact that the capacitances are discharged with the time constant of the system. This way, we can see that the peak on voltage in the different locations changes and that it takes more time to reach the peak as the location is further from the clamp. It also gets shorter as we have already discussed that makes sense.

With that said, we can compute the propagation speed based on these peaks. Computing a liner regression between the time when the peak happens and the distance to the clamp in each location.

Diving the time constant for the space constant we get the propagation speed too:

When applying the current clamp we observe that while in the case of the unmyelinated cable the peak membrane potential decreases significatly with the cable segment, in the myelinated cable it nearly does not change. The value of the membrane potential is almost constant in the whole cable. This leads to the conclusion that in the membrane there is a shortcircuit (or at least a very low impedance connection).

This is reasonable and expected if we consider that the presence of myelin affects the conduction as it is a high resistance, low capacitance layer. Thus, the conduction velocity becomes much bigger. Finally, this increase on conduction velocity leads to a almost constant membrane potential through the cable.
